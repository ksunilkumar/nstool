How to Reduce PDF File Size Online

Large PDF files can be notoriously difficult to share via email due to strict attachment limits (often 25MB or less). Furthermore, uploading large PDFs to web portals can cause timeouts. Our PDF Compressor utilizes advanced optimization algorithms to strip unnecessary metadata and compress embedded raster images inside the document.

Simply select your desired optimization level: Recommended for a great balance between size and readability, Extreme to aggressively downscale images for the absolute smallest file size, or Less to barely compress it but retain maximum print quality. Upload your file, and our system will immediately generate a reduced PDF file, often cutting the file size by over 70% without noticeably reducing the text or image quality.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is this Image and PDF Compressor really free?

Yes! Our platform is 100% free to use. There are no hidden fees, no daily limits, and no watermarks placed on your compressed images or PDFs. We believe essential web utilities should be accessible to everyone.

Are my files safe when compressing online?

Absolutely. Unlike other cloud-based services, our compressor uses modern Web APIs to process your files locally on your own device. Your PDFs and images are never uploaded to any external server, ensuring complete confidentiality for your financial, legal, or personal files.

Can I compress an image to exactly 100KB or 200KB?

Yes! Our Image Compressor includes a unique "Target Size" feature. Just enter your desired KB limit, and the tool will automatically calculate the best compression quality settings to hit that exact size requirement without you having to guess.

Why is my PDF compression taking so long?

Because all processing happens securely inside your browser's memory, extremely large PDFs (over 50MB) may take a few moments to compress depending on your device's processing power. For maximum speed, we recommend using a modern desktop browser.

Does image compression reduce quality?

Image compression works by removing data. "Lossy" compression (like JPEG) will slightly reduce the visual quality, but our smart algorithms ensure that the loss is practically imperceptible to the human eye while dramatically reducing the file size.
